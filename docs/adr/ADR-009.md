# ADR-009: Adopt a Decimal-Based Financial Model with Multi-Currency Support

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** 2026-07-02

---

# Context

WBOS manages financial information including:

* Purchase Orders
* Supplier Costs
* Inventory Valuation
* Sales Invoices
* Customer Payments
* Profit Reporting
* Exchange Rates

Financial calculations require absolute precision.

Small rounding errors can accumulate over thousands of transactions, eventually producing inaccurate reports and reducing trust in the system.

A decision is required regarding how money should be represented throughout WBOS.

---

# Decision

WBOS will adopt a **Decimal-based financial model**.

Every monetary value will use an exact decimal representation.

Floating-point numbers are prohibited for all financial calculations.

The application will support multiple currencies while preserving the original value of every financial transaction.

---

# Financial Philosophy

Money is historical.

Once a financial event occurs, it becomes part of the permanent business record.

Financial history should never change.

Corrections should create new records rather than modifying historical values.

---

# Primary Currency

The operational base currency of WBOS is:

**KWD (Kuwaiti Dinar)**

All business reporting will ultimately be expressed in KWD.

---

# Supported Currencies

Phase 1 supports:

* KWD
* USD
* EUR

The architecture should support additional currencies without database redesign.

Future examples include:

* SAR
* AED
* CNY
* GBP

Currencies should be configuration, not code.

---

# Decimal Representation

Every monetary value must use a Decimal type.

Examples include:

* Unit Cost
* Selling Price
* Discount
* Tax
* Shipping Cost
* Invoice Total
* Payment Amount
* Exchange Rate
* Inventory Valuation
* Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

Floating-point types (`float`, `double`, JavaScript `number` for calculations) must never be used for financial calculations.

---

# Why Decimal?

Decimal arithmetic preserves exact precision.

Example:

```text id="9s83nm"
0.1 + 0.2

Decimal

=

0.3
```

Floating-point arithmetic may produce:

```text id="f1s7wk"
0.30000000000000004
```

Such behavior is unacceptable in business software.

---

# Historical Integrity

Every financial record preserves the values that existed when the transaction occurred.

Historical records should never be recalculated using newer exchange rates or pricing information.

Financial history is immutable.

---

# Original Currency

Every financial record stores its original currency.

Examples:

Purchase Order

Currency: USD

Invoice

Currency: KWD

Supplier Payment

Currency: EUR

The original currency should always be preserved.

---

# Exchange Rates

Whenever currency conversion occurs, WBOS stores:

* Source Currency
* Target Currency
* Exchange Rate
* Effective Date
* Converted Amount

Historical exchange rates must never be modified after use.

Future exchange rate updates affect only future transactions.

---

# Reporting Currency

Although transactions may occur in multiple currencies, reporting requires a consistent basis.

Phase 1 reporting uses:

**KWD**

Reports should convert historical values using the exchange rate captured at the time of the transaction.

Reports should never use today's exchange rate for historical business events.

---

# Financial Precision

WBOS should preserve precision throughout calculations.

Rounding should occur only when presenting values to users or when legally required.

Intermediate calculations should retain full precision.

---

# Rounding Rules

Rounding must be:

* Predictable
* Consistent
* Centralized

The application should define one standard rounding strategy.

No domain should implement custom rounding behavior.

---

# Product Pricing

Products may define default prices in multiple currencies.

However, Invoices preserve a snapshot of the selling price at the time of sale.

Changing a Product's price must never affect historical Invoices.

---

# Purchase Costs

Inventory Lots preserve historical purchase costs.

Each Lot stores:

* Original Unit Cost
* Currency
* Exchange Rate
* Base Currency Cost

Cost history is permanent.

---

# Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

COGS is derived from:

* Inventory Lots
* FIFO consumption
* Historical purchase costs

COGS must never use current Product pricing.

Profit calculations depend on historical costs.

---

# Invoice Totals

Invoice totals are calculated from:

* Line Items
* Discounts
* Taxes
* Shipping Charges (future)

Totals should always be derived.

Manually editing totals is prohibited.

---

# Payments

Payments preserve:

* Original Amount
* Currency
* Exchange Rate (if applicable)
* Payment Date

Payments should never be altered after recording.

Corrections require additional financial records.

---

# Financial Corrections

Mistakes happen.

Financial history should remain truthful.

Incorrect transactions should be corrected using:

* Credit Notes
* Refunds
* Reversals
* Adjustment Entries

Historical financial records should never be silently edited.

---

# Taxes

Phase 1 assumes minimal tax complexity.

The architecture should remain extensible for future support including:

* VAT
* GST
* Sales Tax
* Multiple tax jurisdictions

Tax support should integrate naturally into the financial model.

---

# Currency Conversion

Currency conversion should occur only when necessary.

Conversions should be:

* Explicit
* Traceable
* Reproducible

Every conversion should reference the exchange rate used.

---

# Financial Reporting

Financial reports include:

* Sales
* Purchases
* Gross Profit
* Inventory Valuation
* Customer Balances
* Supplier Balances
* Outstanding Payments

Reports should always be reproducible from historical records.

---

# Why Not Floating Point?

Floating-point numbers introduce:

* Rounding errors
* Inconsistent totals
* Difficult debugging
* Reduced trust

Business software should prioritize correctness over convenience.

---

# Why Not Store Only KWD?

Storing only converted values would lose valuable historical information.

Examples:

* Original supplier invoice
* Original customer invoice
* Exchange rate used
* Purchasing currency

WBOS preserves both original and reporting values.

---

# Alternatives Considered

## Floating Point Arithmetic

Rejected.

Financial precision cannot depend on binary floating-point behavior.

---

## Integer Minor Units Only

Representing money as the smallest currency unit (such as fils or cents) was considered.

Rejected because:

* Different currencies have different decimal precision.
* Exchange rate calculations become more complex.
* Decimal libraries provide a clearer and more expressive model.

This approach may still be appropriate internally for specific integrations if required.

---

## Single-Currency System

Rejected.

The business already purchases goods in multiple currencies.

Future expansion should not require redesign.

---

# Architectural Rules

The following rules apply throughout WBOS.

* Floating-point calculations are forbidden for money.
* Decimal types are mandatory.
* Historical values never change.
* Exchange rates are immutable once used.
* Original currency is always preserved.
* Reports use historical exchange rates.
* Financial calculations remain centralized.
* Rounding follows one shared strategy.

---

# Consequences

This decision provides:

* Financial accuracy
* Reliable reporting
* Multi-currency support
* Historical integrity
* Easier auditing
* Long-term flexibility

The additional implementation effort is justified by the importance of financial correctness.

---

# Future Considerations

Future enhancements may include:

* Automated exchange rate providers
* Multi-currency reporting
* Supplier currency preferences
* Customer currency preferences
* Regional tax engines
* Financial forecasting

These capabilities should extend the existing financial model without requiring redesign.

---

# Review Criteria

This decision should only be reconsidered if:

* Industry standards fundamentally change.
* Financial regulations require a different representation.
* A superior decimal-based approach becomes available.

At present, the chosen model provides the strongest balance of correctness, flexibility, and maintainability.

---

# Final Decision

WBOS will use a Decimal-based financial model with built-in multi-currency support.

Financial history is immutable.

Original currencies are preserved.

Exchange rates are historical facts.

Reports are derived from historical records rather than recalculated using current values.

Money is one of the most sensitive assets managed by WBOS.

Every architectural decision should prioritize correctness, traceability, and trust above convenience or implementation simplicity.
