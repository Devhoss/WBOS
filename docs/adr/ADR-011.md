# ADR-011: Adopt a Business Activity Logging and Audit Trail Strategy

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** 2026-07-02

---

# Context

Business software must explain not only the current state of the business, but also how that state came to be.

Users need to answer questions such as:

* Who changed this?
* When did it happen?
* What changed?
* Why did it change?

---

# Decision

WBOS will maintain an append-only Activity Log for significant business events.

Activity Logs are separate from technical application logs.

---

# Philosophy

Business history is permanent.

Every important action becomes part of the organization's historical record.

Activity Logs exist for accountability rather than debugging.

---

# Examples

Events include:

* Product Created
* Product Archived
* Purchase Order Approved
* Shipment Received
* Invoice Issued
* Payment Recorded
* Customer Updated
* Inventory Adjusted

---

# Stored Information

Every activity should record:

* User
* Organization
* Timestamp
* Event Type
* Target Entity
* Target ID
* Summary
* Optional Metadata

---

# Technical Logs vs Activity Logs

Technical Logs answer:

> Why did the application fail?

Activity Logs answer:

> What happened in the business?

These systems must remain independent.

---

# Immutability

Activity Logs are append-only.

Entries are never edited.

Entries are never deleted.

---

# Privacy

Sensitive information should never be written to Activity Logs.

Passwords, tokens, and secrets must never be stored.

---

# Architectural Rules

* Business events create Activity Logs.
* Activity Logs are immutable.
* Technical logging remains separate.
* Every important action is attributable to a User.

---

# Final Decision

WBOS will maintain a permanent business audit trail that explains the operational history of the organization independently of technical application logging.
