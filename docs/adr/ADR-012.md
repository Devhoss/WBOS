# ADR-012: Adopt a Background Job Architecture for Asynchronous Processing

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** 2026-07-02

---

# Context

Not every operation should execute during an HTTP request.

Some tasks:

* take longer,
* are scheduled,
* or do not require immediate user interaction.

A strategy is required for asynchronous work.

---

# Decision

WBOS will distinguish between:

**Synchronous Business Workflows**

and

**Background Jobs**

Business workflows complete immediately.

Long-running work executes asynchronously.

---

# Phase 1

Background processing will remain intentionally simple.

Examples include:

* Scheduled backups
* Report generation
* Notification delivery
* Email sending
* Data imports
* AI analysis
* Scheduled maintenance

---

# Philosophy

Users should never wait for work that can safely happen later.

However:

Critical business workflows remain synchronous.

Example:

Receiving Inventory

↓

Inventory Updated

↓

Transaction Committed

This should never become an asynchronous workflow.

---

# Job Categories

Examples:

Immediate

* Send Email

Scheduled

* Daily Reports

Recurring

* Database Cleanup

Long Running

* Excel Import

AI

* Demand Forecasting

---

# Future Evolution

Future implementations may introduce:

* Redis
* BullMQ
* Queue Workers
* Distributed Workers

Phase 1 intentionally avoids unnecessary infrastructure.

---

# Architectural Rules

* Business correctness comes before performance.
* Critical workflows remain synchronous.
* Background jobs must be retryable.
* Jobs should be idempotent whenever practical.
* Failures should be observable.

---

# Final Decision

WBOS adopts a simple background job architecture that separates long-running work from critical business workflows while allowing future expansion without redesign.
